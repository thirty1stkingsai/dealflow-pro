Cold Call Script

Opening:
"Hi, is this [Name]? My name is [You] — I'm a local cash home buyer. I came across your property at [Address] and wanted to reach out personally. Do you have 2 quick minutes?"

If Yes:
"I buy homes as-is, all cash — no agents, no commissions, no repairs needed on your end. We can close in as little as 7 days or on your timeline."

Motivation Questions:
"Are there repairs needed?" • "How long have you owned it?" • "What's your ideal timeline?" • "Do you have a price in mind?"

Close:
"I'd love to come take a quick look and put a written offer in your hands — no obligation. Would [day] work for a quick walkthrough?"

How to Explain Wholesale to Sellers

What to Say:

"I'm not an agent, so there are zero commissions to you. I'm a local investor who buys houses directly with cash.

When we agree on a price, I put it in a simple contract. Sometimes I close myself, sometimes I bring in a partner investor — but either way, your price and your closing date don't change. You get exactly what we agreed on, every time.

The benefit to you is speed and simplicity. No showings, no repairs, no waiting on bank approvals, no surprises. You pick a date, we close, you get paid."

If they ask "Are you going to flip it?":
"I might fix it up and sell it, or keep it as a rental — but that's on my end. Your deal stays exactly the same no matter what I do with it after."

Wholesaling Guide

Everything you need to close your first deal

Your Step-by-Step Roadmap
1

Find a Motivated Seller

Use the SC Search or Find tabs. The seller must NEED to sell, not just want to. Look for vacant, pre-FC, tax delinquent, or D4D leads.

2

Analyze the Deal

Use the Calculator. Find ARV (what it's worth fixed up), estimate repairs, calculate your MAO. Your offer must leave room for the investor's profit AND your fee.

3

Make the Offer

Present your cash offer confidently using the Offer Letter generator. You're solving their problem. Most will say no — that's normal. Keep going.

4

Get It Under Contract

Seller accepts? Sign the Purchase & Sale Agreement. Include an assignment clause. Small EMD ($100–$500 to start is fine).

5

Find Your Cash Buyer

Go to the Buyers tab. Present the deal: address, ARV, your price, repairs needed. Serious investors respond within 24–48 hours.

6

Assign the Contract

Use the Assignment of Contract doc. Your buyer pays your assignment fee plus the original contract price. Everyone heads to closing.

7

Close & Collect Your Fee

The title company handles everything. You collect your finder's fee — typically $5,000–$20,000 per deal — at closing.

Staying Legal Without a License
✅ What You CAN Do

✅ Buy and sell real estate you own (your contract is an equitable interest)
✅ Market and sell your CONTRACT — not the property itself
✅ Collect an assignment fee for transferring your contract
✅ Work with a title company for a proper closing
✅ Wholesale in SC without a license if done correctly

⚠️ What to Avoid

❌ Don't advertise the property — you're selling your CONTRACT
❌ Don't act as an agent or represent the seller publicly
❌ Don't collect any fee before closing
❌ Always verify current SC real estate laws — they can change

✅ How to Protect Yourself

✅ Always use written contracts with an assignment clause
✅ Work with a reputable SC title company or real estate attorney
✅ Form an LLC (~$110 in SC — cheap protection)
✅ Always disclose you're an investor, not an agent

⚠️ Disclaimer

This app provides educational information only — not legal advice. SC real estate laws vary. Always consult a licensed SC real estate attorney before conducting wholesale transactions.

How to Wholesale with $0
1

Use Free Lead Sources

PropWire, Zillow, Facebook, Craigslist, D4D, SC County Records — 100% free. You don't need paid lists to start.

2

Keep Your EMD Small

Negotiate a $100–$500 EMD. Or use an inspection contingency — if you can't find a buyer, back out and get your deposit returned.

3

Free Contracts

This app generates purchase agreements, assignments, and authorization letters for free. No attorney fee needed to start.

4

Market for Free

Text, call, Facebook groups, Craigslist, door hangers printed at the library. Your hustle is your marketing budget.

5

Build Your Buyer List First

Go to REIA meetings and Facebook groups NOW before you have a deal. When you find a deal, you'll already know who to call.

Real Talk
